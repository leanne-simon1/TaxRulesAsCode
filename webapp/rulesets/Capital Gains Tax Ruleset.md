1. **Capital gains tax ruleset (calculating the taxable capital gain on disposal of an asset)**

> **Primary-source verified 2026-07-07; tax-specialist review still recommended.**
> Prepared 2026-07-06 from the sources in `research/cgt/README.md`, then verified on
> 2026-07-07 against primary sources: current-law rules against the authoritative ITAA
> 1997 text held locally (Federal Register `C2004A05138`), and the reform against the
> enacted Act text at legislation.gov.au. The reform is a **two-Act package** — the
> **Treasury Laws Amendment (Tax Reform No. 1) Act 2026** (`C2026A00049`, Sch 1 = CGT)
> and the companion **Income Tax Rates Amendment (Tax Reform No. 1) Act 2026**
> (`C2026A00050`), both assented 26 June 2026. Real ITAA 1997 provision numbers are now
> cited (see the verification log in `research/cgt/README.md`). The Explanatory Memorandum has
> also been read (2026-07-07) and every *(VERIFY)* flag resolved. Section 1.6 rules
> are **enacted but not yet commenced** (commence 1 July 2027); section 1.7 items are
> **announced and not yet law**.

   1. **Whether capital gains tax applies at all**

1. Capital gains tax is not a separate tax: the **net capital gain** for the income
   year is included in the entity's assessable income and taxed with the rest of its
   income.[^1]

2. A capital gain or loss can arise only when a **CGT event** happens. The most common
   event is the **disposal** of an asset (event A1), which happens when the **contract**
   is entered into — not at settlement — or, if there is no contract, when ownership
   changes.[^2]

3. A gain or loss on an asset acquired **before 20 September 1985** is disregarded
   entirely — the **pre-CGT (grandfathering) exemption**. It applies until rule 1.6.6
   ends it for gains accruing from 1 July 2027.[^3]

4. Gains and losses on **exempt assets** are disregarded: cars, depreciating assets
   used wholly for a taxable purpose, gambling winnings, and compensation for personal
   injury.[^4]

5. A **collectable** acquired for $500 or less, and a **personal use asset** acquired
   for $10,000 or less, produce no taxable gain. Losses on collectables can offset only
   collectable gains; losses on personal use assets are always disregarded.[^5]

6. A dwelling that was the taxpayer's **main residence** for the whole ownership period
   is fully exempt (partially exempt otherwise), including for up to 6 years of absence
   while it is rented out. A person who is a **foreign resident at the time of the CGT
   event** cannot use the exemption unless a "life events" test is met.[^6]

7. **Death** is not a CGT event for assets passing to the estate or a beneficiary; the
   beneficiary inherits the deceased's cost base (or market value at death for the
   deceased's pre-CGT assets).[^7]

8. A **foreign resident** is subject to CGT only on **taxable Australian property**
   (broadly: Australian real property, indirect interests in it, and business assets of
   an Australian permanent establishment). A 15% withholding applies on relevant
   property disposals from 1 January 2025.[^8]

   2. **Working out the gain or loss on one asset**

1. **Capital gain** = capital proceeds less the asset's **cost base**, where proceeds
   exceed the cost base. **Capital loss** = the asset's **reduced cost base** less the
   proceeds, where proceeds fall short of it.[^9]

2. The **cost base** has five elements: acquisition cost; incidental costs of buying
   and selling; ownership costs; capital improvement costs; and title defence costs.
   Amounts that have been (or can be) claimed as tax deductions are excluded for assets
   acquired after 13 May 1997.[^10]

   3. **Applying losses, then a method (current law, gains realised to 30 June 2027)**

1. Capital losses of the current year, then carried-forward net capital losses (in the
   order they were made), are applied against capital gains **before** any discount or
   indexation. Capital losses can never offset ordinary income; unused losses carry
   forward indefinitely.[^11]

2. **Discount method.** An eligible entity that has held the asset for at least
   **12 months** before the CGT event reduces the remaining gain by the **discount
   percentage**: 50% for individuals and trusts; 33⅓% for complying superannuation
   funds and eligible life insurance companies; **companies get no discount**.[^12]

3. **Foreign and temporary residents** get no discount for gains accruing after
   **8 May 2012**; partial residency periods and gains accrued to that date are
   apportioned.[^13]

4. **Indexation method (legacy).** For an asset acquired at or before 11:45am (ACT
   time) **21 September 1999** and held at least 12 months, the taxpayer may instead
   index the cost base by CPI from acquisition, with the index **frozen at the
   September 1999 quarter**. Indexation cannot create or increase a capital loss. The
   taxpayer chooses whichever of discount or indexation gives the better result.[^14]

   4. **Small business concessions (after the method step)**

1. An entity that is a **CGT small business entity** (aggregated turnover under $2
   million) or satisfies the **$6 million maximum net asset value** test, disposing of
   an **active asset**, may then apply in order: the 15-year exemption (full disregard);
   the 50% active asset reduction; the retirement exemption (up to a $500,000 lifetime
   cap); and/or the small business rollover.[^15]

   5. **The result (current law)**

1. The remaining amount is the **net capital gain**, included in assessable income and
   taxed at the entity's ordinary rate — marginal rates for individuals; 25%/30% for
   companies; 15% in a complying super fund's accumulation phase (effectively 10% on a
   discounted gain); nil in pension phase.[^1]

   6. **Enacted future rules — gains accruing from 1 July 2027**

> Enacted by the Treasury Laws Amendment (Tax Reform No. 1) Act 2026 (`C2026A00049`,
> Schedule 1) and its companion Income Tax Rates Amendment (Tax Reform No. 1) Act 2026
> (`C2026A00050`), both assented 26 June 2026, commencing **1 July 2027**. Applies to
> **individuals, trusts and partnerships**; companies are unaffected and complying
> superannuation funds keep the 33⅓% discount (rule 1.3.2). The main residence
> exemption (1.1.6) is unchanged; the small business concessions (1.4) are largely
> unchanged **except** the 50% active-asset-reduction turnover threshold, which rises
> $2m → $10m (rule 1.6.8, s 152-205).[^16]

1. The **50% discount is abolished** for gains accruing from 1 July 2027 (Division 115 amended).[^16]

2. In its place, for assets held more than 12 months, the cost base is **indexed by
   CPI over the holding period** (no frozen index): tax applies only to the gain above
   the inflation-adjusted cost base. Indexation lifts the cost base only (the reduced
   cost base used for losses is not indexed), so it **cannot create or increase a
   capital loss**; an asset held **under 12 months** gets neither indexation nor the
   discount.[^17]

3. A **30% minimum tax** applies to net capital gains accruing from 1 July 2027, imposed by **Division 119 ITAA 1997** (inserted by the Act; "Minimum rate of tax on capital gains": s 119-5 minimum tax capital gain, s 119-10 extra income tax, s 119-15 exception for recipients of certain income-support payments) — worked as *extra income tax on a minimum tax capital gain* rather than a rate substitution:
   it is worked as a 7-step "minimum tax gap amount" (s 119-10) that tops the gain up
   to 30% of the taxpayer's basic income tax liability (the gain treated as the top
   slice of income, before offsets); the **Medicare levy is not part of the
   calculation** and the rate itself is set by the Rates Act (s 12AA). A taxpayer
   already taxed at 30%+ on the gain pays no extra. **Trust gains are taxed at the
   beneficiary level** (attributed gains under s 115-215 are covered). **Recipients of
   prescribed income-support payments are exempt.**[^18]

4. **Transition — assets already held at 1 July 2027** (**Subdivision 112-E**, inserted
   by the Act: deemed sale and reacquisition just before 1 July 2027; capital-loss
   ordering under s 102-6). The gain that accrued **before** 1 July 2027 is
   **grandfathered** — it keeps the pre-reform treatment under sections 1.1–1.4 —
   while only the gain accruing **from** 1 July 2027 is subject to the amended rules
   in this section. The taxpayer separates the pre- and post-transition gain by
   either (a) a **market valuation** of the asset at 1 July 2027, or (b) a prescribed
   **time-apportionment** method that assumes the gain accrued evenly over the
   ownership period — choosing when lodging the return for the year the asset is
   actually disposed of.[^19]

5. **New builds** (s 115-102) — and, in parallel, **affordable housing** (s 115-125). An investor in a **new build** (a dwelling built on vacant land, or
   replacing demolished dwellings with a greater number) may choose on disposal between
   the **grandfathered 50% discount** (the default for this class) and indexation +
   the 30% minimum.
   The precise **"new residential dwelling" definition is set by Ministerial
   legislative instrument**; the election uses the s 103-25 choice rules and must be
   made **by the day the return is lodged** for the year of disposal; a trustee's choice
   binds the beneficiary.[^20]

6. **Grandfathering for pre-CGT assets ends** (s 112-175). An asset acquired before
   20 September 1985 (a pre-CGT asset, grandfathered under rule 1.1.3) is deemed sold
   on 30 June 2027 and reacquired on 1 July 2027 at **market value** (its reset cost
   base); growth from 1 July 2027 is taxable under the amended rules, while the gain
   accrued to that date remains exempt under the grandfathering treatment. This
   applies to
   **all entities, including companies** (not only individuals, trusts and
   partnerships) — companies keep their no-discount treatment but their pre-CGT assets
   still get the market-value reset.[^21]

7. **Related negative gearing rule (context).** Net rental losses on established
   residential property acquired after 7:30pm AEST 12 May 2026 are quarantined from
   1 July 2027: they carry forward and can offset only rental income or **future
   capital gains from rental property** — an additional source of "capital losses
   applied" in rule 1.3.1 for those assets. Property held before the announcement,
   and new builds, are **grandfathered** and unaffected. In the amended s 102-5
   method statement these quarantined amounts are applied at the inserted
   **Steps 3–4** (against deferred-residential, then
   residential, capital gains) — after capital losses and **before** the discount, and
   only against **residential** capital gains.[^22]

8. **Small business 50% active asset reduction — turnover threshold raised.** For the
   50% active asset reduction only, the aggregated-turnover eligibility threshold rises
   from **$2 million to $10 million** (from the 2027–28 income year). Enacted in this
   Act (s 152-205); the 15-year exemption, retirement exemption and rollover keep the
   existing $2m turnover / $6m net-asset-value tests.[^23a]

   7. **Announced measures — not yet law (do not encode)**

1. **Innovative Business CGT Concession (IBCC)**: a preserved 50% discount for
   founders, employee-share-scheme participants and early-stage investors in innovative
   start-ups (consultation opened 18 June 2026).[^23]

2. A **30% minimum tax on discretionary trusts** from 1 July 2028, with exceptions to
   be detailed (second bill).[^24]

2. **Terms**

| Term | Definition | References |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| CGT event | A transaction or occurrence that can produce a capital gain or loss; event A1 is disposal, timed at contract | Div 104 ITAA 1997 |
| Cost base / reduced cost base | What the asset cost across five elements / the equivalent base used to compute a capital loss | Div 110 ITAA 1997 |
| Discount percentage | The fraction by which an eligible long-held gain is reduced: 50% individuals and trusts (until 30 June 2027, and after that only by new-build election), 33⅓% complying super funds, nil companies | Div 115 ITAA 1997 |
| Indexation | Adjusting the cost base by CPI so only the real gain is taxed — frozen at Sept 1999 under legacy law; restored without freezing for gains accruing from 1 July 2027 | Div 114 ITAA 1997; Tax Reform No. 1 Act 2026 |
| Pre-CGT asset | An asset acquired before 20 September 1985 — **grandfathered** (fully exempt) under current law; the grandfathering ends with a market-value cost base reset at 1 July 2027 under the enacted reform | s 104-10(5) ITAA 1997; Tax Reform No. 1 Act 2026 |
| Net capital gain | The amount included in assessable income after losses, discount/indexation and small business concessions | s 102-5 ITAA 1997 |
| 30% minimum tax | The rate floor on net capital gains accruing from 1 July 2027 (income support recipients exempt) | Tax Reform No. 1 Act 2026 |
| Transition methods | Market valuation at 1 July 2027, or prescribed time apportionment, to split the grandfathered pre-reform gain from the gain taxed under the amended, post-reform rules | Tax Reform No. 1 Act 2026 |

3. **PoC constraints and caveats**

* Scope is the **single-asset disposal calculation** described in
  `research/cgt/rule-inventory.md`; multi-asset aggregation, loss-pool ordering
  choices, partial main-residence arithmetic, foreign-resident apportionment (1.3.3)
  and rollovers other than death are **not** modelled.
* Section 1.6 must be encoded as **dated law** (parameters/formulas gated on the CGT
  event date and gain-accrual date), not as a separate ruleset — one engine, both
  regimes.
* Section 1.7 items must **not** be encoded until enacted; the chatbot should answer
  "announced, not yet law" for them.
* Amending-Act references for rules 1.6.1–1.6.8 are now recorded (Div 119; Subdiv
  112-E / s 112-155, 112-165, 112-175, 112-185; s 102-5/102-6; s 110-36; s 960-275(1B),
  (1C); s 115-102, 115-125; s 152-205), verified against the enacted Act (`C2026A00049`)
  and its Explanatory Memorandum on 2026-07-07 (see the verification log in
  `research/cgt/README.md`). A tax-specialist review of the interpretation is still
  recommended before reliance.
* Outputs are guidance for exploring Rules as Code, not legal or tax advice; any
  user-facing surface must carry the same reliance caveats the ATO's own tools use.
