Working Australians Tax Offset — the $250 WATO

Three ways to use one ruleset. From the 2027-28 income year, an individual who is an Australian resident and whose net labour income (income from work, less the deductions that relate to it) exceeds the $18,200 tax-free threshold gets a tax offset of up to $250 — phasing in at 14 cents per dollar above the threshold. Everything below is computed live by the rules engine from Working Australians Tax Offset Ruleset.md; this page only asks and displays.

Enacted, not yet commenced: the WATO (Treasury Laws Amendment (Tax Reform No. 1) Act 2026, Schedule 3 — new Subdivision 61-E ITAA 1997) applies to assessments for the 2027-28 income year and later. It is non-refundable and applied automatically when your return is assessed — there is nothing to claim.
